**Learning objective:** By the end of this lesson, students will be able to identify and explain key business drivers that encourage organizations to migrate their infrastructure to the cloud.

## What are the potential business drivers?

There are many reasons a business may want to migrate to the cloud. Undoubtedly one of the more common reasons for a business making the move is to reduce IT costs but when we scratch the surface it's not hard to find other reasons to migrate.

<div class="activity discussion">
  <h2 class="title">What reasons might encourage a business to migrate their infrastructure to the cloud?</h2>
  <span class="minutes">10 min</span>
</div>

Let's have a look at some potential business drivers:

| **Business Driver**               | **_Why?_**                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Reduce IT Costs**               | Organizations no longer need large upfront costs to invest in their infrastructure (or renewals/replacements) but instead only pay for what they use.                                                                |
| **Increased Agility**             | The ability to quickly provision on-demand services in the cloud can drastically reduce delivery times of software, allowing business to quickly adapt and get products to market faster than ever before.           |
| **Improved Security**             | The ability to leverage the robust security features offered by cloud providers is invaluable.                                                                                                                       |
| **No End-of-Life Concerns**       | With cloud, the concerns of out of date or broken hardware are passed to the provider. As a consumer you will have access to modern hardware without worrying about what happens when it breaks or becomes outdated. |
| **Consolidation of Data Centers** | No need to maintain your own data centers anymore!                                                                                                                                                                   |
| **Scalability**                   | Instantly scale up and down so that you only use and pay for what you require.                                                                                                                                       |
| **Environmental Benefits**        | Migrating to the cloud can improve energy efficiency by up to **93%** compared to traditional on-premises data centers according to a 2020 Microsoft study.                                                          |

## Case Study

<div class="activity group-exercise">
  <h2 class="title">Discussion: The Downside of an On-Premises Infrastructure</h2>
  <span class="minutes">15 min</span>
</div>

Imagine you are the CEO of a thriving travel agency with branch offices in Taiwan, China, Japan, and the United States.

Your agency generates a significant portion of its revenue—approximately **$30 billion TWD ($974 million USD)**—through its website, where customers explore travel destinations and book their trips online.

However, a major challenge has been the performance of the **on-premises infrastructure** behind your website. Your IT team regularly needs to perform hardware and software updates, which temporarily take the website offline. While you’ve managed these interruptions, a recent incident has raised concerns.

During a recent database upgrade, your website experienced major disruptions lasting **two days**. This downtime resulted in an estimated loss of **$35 million TWD ($1.13 million USD)** in revenue.

How do you prevent this from _ever_ happening again?

### Here are some key points to discuss in your group:

- _What aspects of the current infrastructure may have contributed to the downtime?_

- _How can you minimize customer impact during critical maintenance?_

## What are the Blockers?

There can be many blockers on the road to the cloud and migrations need to be carefully planned and considered.

<br>

Let's have a look at a few potential challenges:

| **Potential Blocker**                   | **_Why_**                                                                                                              |
| --------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Legacy Systems**                      | Are there any existing systems that are holding you back from transitioning?                                           |
| **Skills Required**                     | Does your team have the skills and the new mindset required?                                                           |
| **Regulations**                         | Will you experience problems with regulatory requirements, such as where your clients require their data to be stored? |
| **Cloud Governance and Change Control** | How will you find a balance of business agility and control?                                                           |
| **Ongoing Management**                  | How will you manage day-to-day operations? Will you handle it internally or outsource?                                 |
| **Service Optimization and Monitoring** | How will you monitor and optimize your cloud spending effectively?                                                     |
